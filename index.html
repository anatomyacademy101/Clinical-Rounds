How the activity works

Your group of 5 has two roles: 2 Medical Team members stay on this device the whole time. 3 students rotate as patients, each playing a different character across 5 rounds. After each round, use the ⇄ Rotate Roles tab to hand the patient device to the next student — chart data is never lost.

Procedure
  1. Setup: Enter all 5 group member names above, then go to ⇄ Rotate Roles to assign who plays the first patient. Hand that student a device — they select Patient and pick their room.
  2. Interview: Open Patient Rooms and select the active room. Work through the interview questions, ticking each one as you ask it and recording the patient's answers in the fields.
  3. Vitals & chart: Take the patient's vital signs and record everything in Medical Charts. Use the "✦ Generate Narrative" button to produce a clinical note from the interview.
  4. Diagnosis: Open Disease Reference and use the differential checklist in the chart to eliminate diseases. Narrow to no more than 3, then agree on a final diagnosis.
  5. Record findings: Complete the Final Diagnosis, Evidence, Treatment Plan, and Prognosis fields. Save the chart and confirm the diagnosis in the Team Tracker.
  6. Rotate: When the round is done, the patient clicks ✓ Round Done. On the team device, go to ⇄ Rotate Roles and assign the next patient. Repeat for all 5 rooms.
  7. Infographic: When all 5 rounds are complete, each group member claims one diagnosed disease and creates a patient-facing infographic (see Team Tracker for requirements).

Infographic Assignment

Once all five patients are diagnosed, each of the 5 group members claims one disease from the diagnoses made during rounds and creates a patient-facing infographic. Each infographic must include:

  • Informational overview of the disease
  • Detailed description of signs and symptoms
  • Etiology — causes, risk factors, and mechanisms
  • How the disease is diagnosed (testing methods)
  • Prognosis and long-term outlook for the patient

Each group member should choose a different disease. See your teacher for the submission format.

Rotate Roles

Track who plays each patient and hand off the patient role between rounds. Chart data is never affected by rotation.
